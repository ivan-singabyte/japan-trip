## Transport Card (Do This First)

Get an **IC card** (ICOCA or Suica) per person at Kansai Airport on arrival.
Works on all trains, subways, and buses across Osaka, Kobe, Kyoto.
Tap in, tap out -- no need to buy tickets every time. Load at any convenience store or station machine.

## Day 4 -- Friday, June 5 | Kobe Day Trip + Arima Onsen

**Check out Osaka Airbnb by 11am**
**Arima Onsen check-in: 3pm**
**Kobe is 30 min from Osaka by train, Arima Onsen is 30 min from Kobe**

### Option A: Kobe Lunch Stop (Recommended)
- Store luggage at Shin-Kobe Station lockers before exploring
- **Kobe Kitano-cho:** European-style district, Western-style historic mansions (Ijinkan)
  - Fun walk, about 1 hr to see 2-3 houses, kids enjoy the quirky architecture
- **Kobe Beef Lunch:** This is the moment -- you are eating at the source
  - Recommend: Kobe Beef at Steakland Kobe (affordable set lunches ~4,000-6,000 yen per adult)
  - Or: Mouriya Sannomiya (mid-range, great for families)
  - Book ahead -- popular restaurants fill up fast
- 2:00pm: Take bus from Shin-Kobe to Arima Onsen (~30 min)
- 3:00pm: Check into Arima Gyoen

### Option B: Skip Kobe, Go Straight to Arima
- If the kids are exhausted after 2 days at USJ, just head directly to Arima
- You get more time at the ryokan, can use the baths earlier
- Buy Kobe beef snacks at the ryokan or on the walk around Arima village

**At Arima Gyoen Ryokan:**
- Check-in includes: Kobe beef multi-course kaiseki dinner and traditional breakfast
- **Two types of hot springs:**
  - Kinsen (Gold Spring): reddish-brown, iron-rich, good for skin
  - Ginsen (Silver Spring): colourless, carbonate mineral water
- First-time onsen tips for the family:
  - Wash and rinse thoroughly before entering the baths
  - Tattoos are not allowed in public baths (private baths usually fine)
  - No swimwear -- this is traditional (separate baths for men and women)
  - Kids 11 and 14 are old enough to manage separately with a parent
- Walk Arima village after dinner (small but charming, traditional shops)

**Check out Arima Onsen by 10am**

### Arriving in Kyoto Before 3pm:

### Option A: Fushimi Inari + Nishiki Market
- **Nishiki Market** (Kyoto's Kitchen) -- covered market, great street food
  - Yuba (tofu skin), matcha soft serve, fresh mochi, pickles
  - 15 min walk from Kyoto Station
- **Fushimi Inari Taisha** -- the famous thousands of orange torii gates
  - Free entry, open 24 hours
  - Lower trail (30-45 min walk) is easy and spectacular

  - Gets crowded by 10am -- arriving around noon is fine on a Sat
  - Fox statues everywhere -- kids find them cool

### Option B: Gion District + Philosopher's Path
- Walk Hanamikoji Street in Gion (wooden machiya townhouses, possible geisha spotting in late afternoon)
- Stroll along the **Philosopher's Path** canal -- peaceful 2km walk lined with trees
- Stop at **Nanzenji Temple** (massive wooden gate, beautiful grounds, free to enter outer area)

**3pm: Check into Kyoto Airbnb (40 Bishamoncho)**
**Evening:** Walk the neighbourhood, light dinner. Gion has great ramen and yakitori spots.

## Leg 1: KIX to Osaka Airbnb (Fukushima-ku), Jun 2

**Fastest (recommended): Haruka Express + one Loop Line stop, ~55 min total**
1. After immigration and baggage, follow signs to "Railways / JR." Buy your ICOCA cards at the JR machines, plus a discounted Haruka one-way ticket (1,800 yen to the Osaka area with your passport).
2. Board the **Haruka Express** toward Osaka/Kyoto. Ride to **Osaka Station** (the Umekita underground platform), about 50 min.
3. At Osaka Station, transfer to the **JR Osaka Loop Line** and go one stop to **Fukushima Station** (~3 min).
4. Exit and walk to the "8-14-7" pin. Use your Airbnb map for the last 5 to 10 min.

**Cheaper: Kansai Airport Rapid, ~70 min, ~1,210 yen**
Take the **JR Kansai Airport Rapid (Kanku Kaisoku)** toward Osaka. It runs onto the Loop Line; ride to Fukushima Station (or change at Osaka Station for one stop if your train terminates there). No reserved seat, but slower with luggage.

Tip: if your bags are heavy, the Haruka has luggage racks and is far more comfortable for four people.

## Leg 2: Osaka to Kobe, then up to Arima Onsen, Jun 5

You check out by 11am, do Kobe lunch, then ride up to Arima for the 3pm ryokan check-in.

**Osaka (Fukushima) to Kobe-Sannomiya**

Simplest, one train, no transfer:
1. Walk to **Hanshin Fukushima Station** (Hanshin Main Line).
2. Board a train toward **Kobe-Sannomiya** (Fukushima is the first stop after Osaka-Umeda, so most Kobe-bound trains stop here). Ride to **Kobe-Sannomiya**, roughly 35 to 40 min, about 330 yen.

Faster JR alternative:
1. JR Fukushima to **Osaka Station** (1 Loop Line stop).
2. Transfer to the **JR Special Rapid (Kobe Line)** to **Sannomiya**, ~22 min, ~420 yen. Total from Fukushima ~30 to 35 min.

Lunch and a stroll around Sannomiya/Kitano (Kobe beef sets, Chinatown), then store or carry bags for the climb to Arima.

**Kobe-Sannomiya to Arima Onsen (recommended: direct bus)**
1. Go to the **Sannomiya Bus Terminal** at Mint Kobe (right by Sannomiya Station).
2. Board the **JR Bus "Arima Express"** direct to **Arima Onsen**, ~30 min, ~780 yen. No transfers, easiest with luggage and kids.
3. From the Arima Onsen bus stop, Arima Gyoen is a short walk into the village. The ryokan can advise on its exact entrance, and some ryokan offer a pickup from the bus/station, so it is worth asking when you confirm.

Train alternative (more transfers): Sannomiya Subway (Seishin-Yamate Line) to **Tanigami**, transfer to the **Kobe Electric Railway** to **Arimaguchi**, then change to the Arima branch to **Arima Onsen** station. Similar ride time but with two changes.

## Leg 3: Arima Onsen to Kyoto, Jun 6

Check out by 10am; Kyoto check-in is 3pm, so you have a relaxed morning.

**Easiest (recommended): direct highway bus, ~70 to 75 min, ~1,850 yen**
1. From the Arima Onsen bus terminal, take the **direct highway bus to Kyoto** (operated by Hankyu/Keihan-affiliated lines). It runs to **Kyoto Station** (Hachijo side).
2. Reserve in advance, as seats are limited and it is the most comfortable option with bags. Confirm the timetable when you check in at the ryokan, since this is a limited service and schedules shift seasonally.

**Train backup (if the bus is full or not running that day)**
1. Bus or Kobe Electric Railway from Arima down to **Sannomiya** or **Shin-Kobe**.
2. From **Sannomiya**, JR Special Rapid to **Kyoto**, ~1 hr, ~1,110 yen. (Or Shinkansen from **Shin-Kobe** to Kyoto, ~30 min but pricier, around 2,870 yen.)

From Kyoto Station to the Bishamonchō Airbnb: if it is in the Higashiyama/Gion area as expected, it is a short taxi (~1,200 to 1,800 yen) or a city bus ride. Confirm the exact pin in your app and you can hand a taxi driver the address. Drop bags or use Kyoto Station coin lockers if you arrive before the 3pm check-in.

## Leg 4: Kyoto to KIX, Jun 9 (6pm flight)

**Haruka Express, direct, ~80 min, ~3,060 yen (or ~2,200 discount)**
1. Check out by 10am. With an international flight you want to be at KIX about 3 hours early, so aim to leave Kyoto Station by roughly 1:30pm.
2. At **Kyoto Station**, board the **Haruka Express** toward Kansai Airport. It is a direct, no-transfer ride to **KIX**, about 75 to 80 min. Trains run roughly every 30 min.
3. Reserve seats (especially with luggage), or use a non-reserved car. The discounted one-way foreigner ticket to KIX is about 2,200 yen with your passport.
4. At KIX, the Haruka arrives right at the airport station; follow signs up to departures.

If you want a last Osaka stop instead, you could ride to Namba and take the Nankai line to KIX, but the direct Haruka from Kyoto is simpler and lower-risk on a flight day.
