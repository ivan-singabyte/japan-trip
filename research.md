# Kansai Family Trip: Osaka, Kobe/Arima Onsen & Kyoto — June 2 to 9, 2026
**Expanded Day-by-Day Itinerary for 2 Adults + 2 Kids (ages 14 and 11)**
Focus: middle-class casual eats, cafes, and bookshops, with hard 2026 data.

## TL;DR
- This 8-day Kansai loop is well-structured: 3 nights Osaka (Fukushima-ku), 1 night Arima Onsen ryokan, 3 nights Kyoto (Bishamoncho), bookended by KIX. The plan below adds 40+ named, currently-operating restaurants, cafes, and bookshops plus exact 2026 prices, hours, and station data.
- Biggest action items: buy ICOCA cards on arrival at KIX, pre-book a USJ Express Pass (it is the 25th-anniversary year and crowds are at a decade high), and reserve the Arima ryokan dinner. June is tsuyu (rainy season), so build in indoor backups (cafes, bookshops, museums).
- Budget guidance: most meals 800 to 2,500 yen per person; a few splurges (Kobe beef lunch ~3,000 to 5,800 yen, ryokan kaiseki included). Avoid luxury kaiseki/teppanyaki priced 10,000 yen-plus.

## Key Findings
- **USJ in 2026 is exceptionally busy.** The park's 25th anniversary falls on March 31, 2026 (USJ opened March 31, 2001), and the year-long "Discover U!!!" celebration runs Wednesday March 4, 2026 through Tuesday March 30, 2027 (with the "NO LIMIT! Parade – Discover U!!! Version" debuting March 4). Combined with the new Donkey Kong Country, the free Super Nintendo World timed-entry lottery in the app can sell out by ~10:15 to 10:30 AM. With a 3pm-onward day (June 3) and a full day (June 4), an Express Pass that includes guaranteed area entry is strongly recommended for at least one day.
- **ICOCA + Haruka combo ended September 30, 2023** (confirmed by JR West). Buy a standalone ICOCA at KIX and a discounted "Haruka One-way" ticket separately.
- **June is rainy season** in Kansai (early June to mid-July), ~19 to 28°C, high humidity, roughly 10 to 12 rain days but rarely all-day downpours. Pack light rain gear and plan indoor fallbacks.
- **Cash still matters**: many small Kyoto/Nara shops and Nakatanidou (mochi) are cash-only; IC cards and credit cards work in chains and stations.
- **Bishamoncho, Kyoto** is in Higashiyama near Gion/Kiyomizudera, ideal for the temple cluster and evening Gion/Pontocho strolls.

### Practical Logistics (2026)

**IC Cards (ICOCA):** Buy at KIX or any JR station vending machine. Card costs ~2,000 yen (1,500 yen credit + 500 yen deposit); child ICOCA available (auto child fares). Usable on all Kansai trains, subways, buses, convenience stores, vending machines, coin lockers. Refundable at a JR ticket office (500 yen deposit back minus 220 yen handling fee), or keep it (valid ~10 years). Physical cards are available in Kansai in 2026.

**Haruka Express (KIX to city):** ~50 min to Osaka Station, ~75 min to Kyoto. Regular Kyoto fare is 3,060 yen non-reserved. The foreigner-only "HARUKA One-way Discount Ticket" (Temporary Visitor status, passport required) is tiered: **Kyoto 2,200 yen, Kobe 2,000 yen, Osaka/Shin-Osaka 1,800 yen, Tennoji 1,300 yen.** New for 2026: a WEST QR e-ticket removes the voucher-exchange queue. Trains run ~every 30 min, ~6:30am to ~10pm.

**Weather/packing (June):** Light breathable clothes, a packable rain jacket/poncho, compact umbrella (or buy a 500 yen one at any konbini), quick-dry footwear, a light layer for strong A/C, and a handheld fan. Bring a small bag for wet items; humidity breeds mildew.

**Connectivity:** Pocket wifi and SIM/eSIM available at KIX arrivals. An eSIM (bought before travel) is most convenient per person; one pocket wifi can cover the whole group.

**Cash vs card 2026:** Cards/IC widely accepted in chains, department stores, USJ, big restaurants. Carry cash for small eateries, markets (Nishiki, Kuromon), temples, Nakatanidou, and Arima's smaller shops. 7-Bank ATMs (in 7-Eleven) accept foreign cards.

### DAY 1 — Tuesday June 2: Arrive KIX, settle in Osaka (Fukushima-ku)
- Land KIX 8am. Clear immigration, buy ICOCA cards, grab eSIM/pocket wifi. Take Haruka to Osaka Station (~50 min) then one stop/short ride to Fukushima. (Airbnb check-in 4pm, so store bags or explore.)
- Fukushima-ku is one JR stop from Osaka Station/Umeda, a relaxed local foodie neighborhood (great for dinners back near "home").
- Late morning/lunch near Umeda or Fukushima; afternoon easy exploring, then check in 4pm.

**Eat — Fukushima-ku & Umeda (near your base):**
- **Fukushima izakaya alley (Fukumaru Dori 57):** lively strip of casual izakaya plus a Hankyu Oasis supermarket with a 2nd-floor eat-in space (self-serve coffee), 3-min walk from JR Fukushima Station. Good low-key first night.
- **Sobakiri Karani** (Fukushima): handmade soba, mackerel/duck toppings; quiet, quality. ~1,500 to 2,500 yen.
- **POGO** (Fukushima): friendly craft-beer izakaya with small plates from ~280 yen, pizza/pasta; relaxed for families early evening.
- **Hanakujira** (Fukushima): oden specialist, comforting and cheap.
- Quick/affordable: **551 Horai** butaman (pork buns), in Umeda department-store food halls — an Osaka institution, great grab-and-go for tired kids.

**Cafe:** Department-store depachika under Hanshin/Hankyu Umeda for pastries and easy first-day snacks.

**Bookshop (Umeda, near Osaka Station):**
- **MARUZEN & Junkudo Umeda** (7-20 Chayamachi, Kita-ku; ~10:00 to 22:00): one of Japan's largest bookstores, 7 floors B1 to 7F, big foreign-language/English selection. Excellent rainy-evening browse.
- **Umeda Tsutaya Books** (9F Lucua 1100, 3-min from Hankyu Umeda; 10:30 to 21:00): stylish, 500+ seats, children's book corner with tables on 7F.

### DAY 2 — Wednesday June 3: Osaka sightseeing, then USJ from 3pm
- Morning/midday: Osaka Castle and/or Kuromon Market and Dotonbori, then head to USJ for the 3pm-onward entry.
- **Osaka Castle:** main keep open 9:00 to 17:00 (last entry 16:30); grounds open 24h and free. Adult ~600 yen; kids (under 15) free. Less crowded right at 9am. Nearest: Tanimachi 4-chome or Osakajokoen stations. ~1 to 1.5 hr for the keep/museum.
- **Kuromon Ichiba Market:** ~150 to 180 stalls, "Osaka's Kitchen"; go before 11am for the best atmosphere. Fresh sashimi sticks, grilled scallops/crab, tamagoyaki, fruit. Near Nipponbashi/Namba.
- **USJ from 3pm:** Use the app to grab a Super Nintendo World timed-entry as soon as you enter (or rely on your Express Pass slot). With only an evening, prioritize Super Nintendo World (Mario Kart: Koopa's Challenge) and one Harry Potter ride.

**USJ DEEP DETAIL (2026):**
- **1-Day Studio Pass:** Adult ~8,600 to 10,900 yen depending on date; Child ~5,400 to 6,200 yen (per Japan Cheapo's 2026 guide). Buy online (Klook/KKday/official) to skip queues; check the date price calendar.
- **Express Pass:** add-on that does NOT include admission. Express Pass 7 is the recommended all-rounder, from ~21,800 yen; Express Pass 8 from ~23,900 yen. Only Express Pass Premium guarantees both Super Nintendo World and Donkey Kong Country entry. Admission + Express combined "can easily reach around 19,000 to 40,000+ yen" depending on date and tier.
- **Super Nintendo World / Donkey Kong Country (opened Dec 2024):** require an Area Timed Entry Ticket (free via app, or guaranteed via certain Express Passes). From Jan 5, 2026 the official app is required for timed/standby tickets. App slots can be gone by ~10:15 to 10:30 AM on busy days.
- **Height limits relevant to ages 11 & 14:** both kids clear the key thresholds. Mine Cart Madness (Donkey Kong) needs 107 cm with an adult or 122 cm solo; thrill coasters like Flying Dinosaur require ~132 cm. Your 11 and 14 year-olds can ride everything.
- **June weekday hours:** typically open 8:00 or 9:00 to ~21:00 (varies; the park frequently opens earlier than published). Check the official calendar; arrive at the gate at least an hour before posted opening.
- **Food inside:** Kinopio's (Toad's) Cafe in Super Nintendo World (reserve via app), Mario Cafe & Store (Hollywood, pancake sandwiches), plus counter spots; sit-down lines can hit 60 to 90 min at lunch — eat early or late.
- **Strategy:** Day 1 (evening) hit Nintendo + Harry Potter with Express; Day 2 (full day) relax with Minion Park, Jurassic Park, shows, and re-rides.

**Eat near USJ / Universal CityWalk:** chain and themed restaurants on CityWalk; in-park options as above. For a cheaper meal, eat in the city before entering.

**Eat in Osaka (if pre-USJ lunch in Dotonbori):**
- **Dotonbori takoyaki:** Takoyaki Doraku Wanaka, Honke Ootako (oldest, since 1972, Michelin Bib mention; giant octopus), Kukuru (creamy), Acchichi Honpo (riverside). ~600 to 1,000 yen for a tray.
- **Okonomiyaki:** Chibo (Dotonboriyaki) or casual Bonkuraya; Mizuno is a 75-year favorite. ~900 to 1,800 yen.
- **Kushikatsu:** Kushikatsu Daruma or Kushikatsu Shirotaya (Dotonbori); ~100 to 200 yen/skewer, no double-dipping in the communal sauce.

### DAY 4 — Friday June 5: Check out Osaka, travel to Arima Onsen via Kobe
- Check out 11am. Store bags / forward luggage. Train to Kobe-Sannomiya (JR Special Rapid ~22 min, ~420 yen). Lunch + brief Kobe sightseeing, then bus/train up to Arima Onsen (ryokan check-in 3pm).
- **Kobe options before Arima:** Kitano Ijinkan (Western-style houses; Kitano-cho), Nankinmachi Chinatown (street food), Sannomiya shopping. Then JR Bus "Arima Express" Sannomiya to Arima Onsen (~30 min, ~780 yen).

**Eat — Kobe (affordable Kobe beef lunch & local):**
- **Steakland Kobe** (Sannomiya): popular teppanyaki Kobe beef lunch sets ~3,000 yen; expect a line.
- **Kobe Steak Propeller** (5-min from Sannomiya): casual, cozy; Kobe Steak S lunch (100g) ~3,000 yen + ~500 yen set.
- **Hiroshige** (Sannomiya): Kobe beef gyudon (beef bowl) ~1,500 yen; tiny (≈9 to 10 seats), often a wait, but the cheapest way to taste Kobe beef.
- **Kobe Tanryu** (Sannomiya): akami (lean) lunch set ~2,500 yen + tax; English menu.
- **Nagata Tank Suji** (Sannomiya, Sun Plaza B1F): Kobe soul food sobameshi + beef-tendon "bokkake" ~800 to 1,500 yen; retro, fun, cheap.
- **Nankinmachi (Chinatown) street food:** Roushouki butaman (pork buns since 1915), Yunyun fried soup dumplings, panda buns, Kobe beef croquettes. ~200 to 800 yen each. Near Motomachi Station.

**Cafe — Kobe:**
- **Freundlieb** (Kitano): historic German bakery-cafe in a 1929 former church; egg/roast-beef sandwiches, strawberry shortcake; ~1,200 to 2,200 yen; closed Wednesdays. Atmospheric brunch en route to Kitano.
- **Nishimura Coffee** — classic Kobe coffee house since 1948.
- Kobe is famous for chocolate; nearly a dozen chocolatiers within 10 min of Sannomiya.

**Bookshop — Kobe:**
- **Junkudo Sannomiya** — large multi-floor store right by Sannomiya Station (the chain's historic Kobe flagship), some English.

**ARIMA ONSEN (afternoon/evening) — village detail:**
- One of Japan's three oldest hot springs (~1,300 years), Kita-ku, Kobe. Two waters: **Kinsen** ("gold," iron/salt, reddish-brown) and **Ginsen** ("silver," clear, carbonated/radium).
- **Public baths** (worth it even though your ryokan has its own):
  - **Kin no Yu** (gold): adults ~650 yen weekday / ~800 yen weekend, ~9:00 to 21:00 (last entry 20:30), closed 2nd & 4th Tue; free footbath (Taiko no Ashiyu) and drinking fountain outside.
  - **Gin no Yu** (silver): adults ~550 weekday / ~700 weekend, ~9:00 to 21:00, closed 1st & 3rd Tue.
  - Combination ticket for both ~1,200 yen.
  - **Taiko no Yu** (Arima View Hotel): big bath "theme park," both waters + sauna; ~2,750 yen (2,970 weekends/holidays).
- **Onsen etiquette (kids/tattoos):** wash thoroughly before entering; no swimsuits; tie long hair up; no towels in the water; kids must be supervised and quiet. Most public baths restrict tattoos. Kin no Yu is noted as relatively tattoo-friendly, but for anyone with tattoos the safest bet is your ryokan's private/family bath or covering with patches.
- **Things to do in the village:** stroll Yumotozaka and Tansan-zaka slopes; Taiko no Yudonokan museum (Hideyoshi's excavated bathhouse); Onsen-ji Temple; Nene-bashi/Taiko-bashi bridges; the carbonated-spring park; Arima Toys and Automata Museum (good for kids).
- **Food specialties & shops:** **tansan senbei** (carbonated crackers), **Arima cider**, onsen manju. **Arima Tansanriki** shop (in front of Kin no Yu footbath) sells novelty ciders (gyoza cider, curry-bread cider, etc.) — fun with kids.
- Dinner: your ryokan (Arima Gyoen) kaiseki is typically included — confirm when booking.

### DAY 5 — Saturday June 6: Arima morning, travel to Kyoto (Bishamoncho)
- Ryokan check-out 10am. Optional morning soak/footbath and a final stroll + souvenir crackers. Then direct highway bus Arima to Kyoto (~70 to 75 min, ~1,850 yen) OR train via Shin-Kobe. Kyoto Airbnb (Bishamoncho, Higashiyama) check-in 3pm.
- Afternoon in Higashiyama near your base: Kiyomizudera + Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka, then dinner in Gion.

**Kyoto attractions (2026 hours/prices):**
- **Kiyomizudera:** ~6:00 to 18:00 (seasonal); admission ~500 yen. Go early or late afternoon to dodge crowds. The Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka lanes below are lined with shops and cafes.
- **Fushimi Inari Taisha:** free, open 24h; JR Inari station (5 min, 150 yen from Kyoto Stn). Best very early morning or evening; allow 1.5 to 3 hr (to Yotsutsuji viewpoint ~30 to 45 min up).
- **Kinkakuji (Golden Pavilion):** ~9:00 to 17:00; ~500 yen.
- **Ryoanji (rock garden):** ~8:00 to 17:00; ~600 yen.
- **Nijo Castle:** ~8:45 to 17:00 (last entry ~16:00), closed some Tuesdays; ~1,300 yen (castle + Ninomaru Palace).
- **Ginkakuji + Philosopher's Path + Nanzenji:** Ginkakuji ~8:30 to 17:00, ~500 yen; the Path is free; Nanzenji grounds free (sub-temples/aqueduct paid).

**Eat — Gion/Higashiyama dinner:**
- **Gion Tanto** (Miyagawasuji, near Gion-Shijo): okonomiyaki/teppanyaki cooked at your table; ~1,500 to 3,000 yen; English menu; vegan okonomiyaki options. Fun for kids.
- **Mimikou** (Higashiyama): Kyoto curry udon; ~1,000 to 1,500 yen; small, popular.
- **Kyou-no-Jidoriya Aki**: Kyoto local-chicken; lunch ramen ~850 yen.

**Cafe — Higashiyama/Gion:**
- **Maeda Coffee** (kissaten, near Yasaka Pagoda): house-roasted coffee, matcha mousse, parfaits; two roomy floors, rarely too busy.
- **Saryo Tsujiri / Gion Tsujiri** (Gion): famous Uji-matcha parfaits (Tokusen Tsujiri Parfait); expect a line.
- **MACCHA HOUSE Matcha-kan** (Sannenzaka): matcha tiramisu in a wooden masu.
- **% Arabica Higashiyama** — minimalist specialty coffee, scenic but busy.

### DAY 6 — Sunday June 7: Arashiyama day
- Morning to Arashiyama (JR Saga-Arashiyama from Kyoto Station ~15 min, ~240 yen). Beat crowds: bamboo grove before 8:30am if possible.
- **Bamboo Grove (Chikurin no Komichi):** free, open 24h; ~400 m path, ~15 to 20 min walk. Enter via Tenryuji's north gate for the quieter end.
- **Tenryuji:** ~8:30 to 17:00; garden ~500 yen (600 yen combo with Gioji). UNESCO Zen temple with a beautiful garden.
- **Iwatayama Monkey Park:** 9:00 to 16:30 (last entry ~16:00); adult ~800 yen, child (4 to 15) ~400 yen; cash only. ~20 min uphill hike to ~120 wild macaques + Kyoto panorama. Kids love feeding them (from inside the hut). Keep 2 m distance, no eye contact, no touching.
- Optional: Okochi Sanso Villa (~1,000 yen incl. matcha + sweet), Togetsukyo Bridge.

**Eat — Arashiyama:**
- Yudofu (simmered tofu) is the Arashiyama specialty — several traditional houses near Tenryuji; lunch sets ~2,000 to 3,000 yen.
- Casual: shichirin-grilled dango/sweets stalls along Arashiyama main street; soba/udon shops; matcha soft-serve.

**Cafe — Arashiyama:**
- **% Arabica Arashiyama** (riverside, by Togetsukyo): excellent coffee, spectacular view; often a line.
- **Saryo Suisen** / **Nakamura Tokichi** (Uji-matcha desserts) and **Okochi Sanso** tea house (matcha in the garden).

**Bookshop note:** Arashiyama is light on bookshops — save book time for Day 7 (Ichijoji/central).

### DAY 7 — Monday June 8: Kyoto book day + central sights (or Nara day trip alternative)
This is the literary-lover's day. Two options: (A) a curated Kyoto bookshop + central sights day, or (B) a Nara day trip. Given the book-lover emphasis, Option A is recommended; Nara can swap in if the family prefers deer/Big Buddha.

**Option A — Kyoto bookshops & central sights:**
- **Keibunsha Ichijoji** (near Ichijoji Stn, Eizan line; ~10:00 to 21:00): selected by The Guardian in 2010 as one of its "Top 10 most beautiful bookstores in the world" — the only Japanese store on the list. Curated Japanese art/lit, gallery, stationery, cafe. A pilgrimage for book lovers; small English selection but gorgeous to browse.
- **Hohohoza Jodoji** (near Ginkakuji): quirky indie bookshop with manga, philosophy, handmade toys — good combined with a Ginkakuji/Philosopher's Path morning.
- **Seikosha** (near Kawaramachi-Marutamachi): refined, owner-curated (ex-Keibunsha manager).
- **Maruzen Kyoto** (B1-B2 of Kyoto BAL, Kawaramachi; the literary setting of the novel "Lemon"): big foreign-language section (27 racks), Maruzen Cafe with a Kyoto-only lemon cake (~700 yen).
- **Kyoto International Manga Museum** (Karasuma-Oike; **10:30 to 17:30, admission to 17:00**, closed Wednesdays): adults 1,200 yen, ages 13 to 18 400 yen, ages 6 to 12 200 yen. Collection of ~300,000 items (as of 2016) with 50,000 volumes readable on the open-access "Wall of Manga" spanning ~200 meters across three floors (shonen 1F, shojo 2F, seinen 3F). Kids can sit and read for hours. ~2 to 3 hr. June 8, 2026 is a Monday, so it is open.
- **Nishiki Market** for lunch grazing (see eats).

**Eat — central Kyoto (Pontocho/Kawaramachi/Nishiki):**
- **Gyoza Chao Chao** (Sanjo-Kiyamachi & Shijo-Kawaramachi): bite-size gyoza, 16 pcs ~600 to 670 yen; chicken-wing/cheese/yuba (vegan) gyoza; value sets ~1,490 to 1,700 yen; lively, English menu, vegetarian options; expect a 30 to 60 min queue.
- **Ichiran Kyoto-Kawaramachi:** customizable tonkotsu ramen in solo booths; base ~980 yen, with toppings ~1,200 to 1,800 yen; novelty kids enjoy.
- **Gyukatsu Kyoto Katsugyu** (Pontocho): grill-your-own breaded beef cutlet; ~1,500 to 2,500 yen.
- **Ramen Sen-no-Kaze:** Kyoto-style pork-salt ramen, big menu; ~900 to 1,400 yen.
- **Nishiki Market grazing:** Kai (tako tamago), Miki Keiran (dashimaki tamago), Konna Monja (soy doughnuts), yuba croquettes, beef croquettes, strawberry daifuku; ~200 to 1,200 yen. Eat at the stall (no walking-and-eating); many shops closed Wednesdays.

**Eat — Kyoto Station (if back via station):**
- **Kyoto Ramen Koji** (10F Kyoto Station): 8 regional ramen shops; ~700 to 1,500 yen; everyone picks a region; vending-machine tickets.
- **Katsukura** (Kyoto Station, The Cube 11F / Porta; also Sanjo Honten): Kyoto tonkatsu with free cabbage/rice/miso refills; sets ~1,800 to 2,500 yen; great for hungry teens.

**Cafe — central Kyoto:**
- **Smart Coffee** (Teramachi, since 1932): hand-roasted coffee, fluffy hotcakes, French toast — classic kissaten.
- **Inoda Coffee Honten** (since 1940): nostalgic European-style kissaten, breakfast sets and parfaits.
- **Salon de Thé François** (since 1934, near Pontocho): Tudor-style cafe, classic cakes.
- **% Arabica / Weekenders Coffee / Kurasu** for specialty third-wave.

**Option B — Nara day trip (alternative):**
- Kyoto Station to Nara: Kintetsu Express ~45 min, ~760 yen (Kintetsu Nara is closer to the park) or JR Miyakoji Rapid ~45 min, ~720 yen.
- **Nara Park:** free, open 24h; ~1,200 to 1,300 free-roaming deer. Deer crackers (shika senbei) ~200 yen. Kids: feed calmly, keep crackers hidden until ready.
- **Todai-ji (Great Buddha):** ~7:30/8:00 opening; adult ~800 yen; the ~15 m bronze Daibutsu in the world's largest wooden hall. Don't miss the "enlightenment" pillar hole kids can crawl through.
- **Kasuga Taisha** (brass lanterns) and **Kofukuji** pagoda nearby; **Naramachi** old merchant town.

**Eat — Nara:**
- **Hiraso** (3-min from Kintetsu Nara): kakinoha-zushi (persimmon-leaf sushi) sets ~1,500 to 2,500 yen; takeaway boxes for a park picnic.
- **Kakinohazushi Hompo Tanaka** (outside Kintetsu Nara): persimmon-leaf sushi packs ~600 to 1,500 yen, grab-and-go.
- **Tengyokudo** (near Todai-ji): kuzu (arrowroot) udon and kuzumochi desserts; ~900 to 1,600 yen.
- Try **Miwa somen** (cold in June) at Naramachi eateries / Izasa sets ~1,680 yen.

**Cafe/sweets — Nara:**
- **Nakatanidou** (Higashimuki arcade, ~5 min from Kintetsu Nara): famous high-speed mochi-pounding show; yomogi mochi ~150 to 200 yen; cash only; show ~every 30 min, winds down ~3pm. Kids love it.
- **Kanakana** (Naramachi machiya cafe): home-style set "Kanakana Gohan" ~1,300 to 1,800 yen; cash; go early.
- **Mahoroba Daibutsu Pudding** (Nara Park / Naramachi): custard puddings from ~350 yen.

## Recommendations
1. **Book now (before the trip):** USJ 1-Day Studio Pass for both June 3 and 4 (or a 1.5/2-day pass), plus an Express Pass 7 (from ~21,800 yen) — or Premium for guaranteed Nintendo + Donkey Kong — for at least the full day June 4. Prices and Express dates sell out in the 25th-anniversary year, so secure ASAP.
2. **On arrival at KIX:** buy 4 ICOCA cards (child cards for the 11-year-old if eligible) and a discounted Haruka 1-Way ticket (1,800 yen to Osaka, or buy a Kyoto one for the return leg at 2,200 yen); set up eSIM/pocket wifi.
3. **Reserve:** confirm the Arima Gyoen ryokan dinner/kaiseki; reserve Kinopio's Cafe via the USJ app on the day; expect queues (not reservations) at Gyoza Chao Chao, Katsukura, Steakland, and Nakatanidou — arrive at opening.
4. **Rainy-season plan:** keep indoor backups ready each day — Manga Museum, Maruzen/Junkudo, department-store depachika, kissaten, Kuromon/Nishiki markets, Arima museums.
5. **Pacing for kids:** front-load big walking (temples, monkey park, Fushimi Inari) in cooler mornings; use afternoons for cafes, bookshops, and markets. Carry water and a fan; June humidity is real. Cold somen, conveyor sushi, and kakigori (shaved ice) are good heat-beaters.
6. **Decision thresholds:** If USJ app timed-entry is still available when you arrive (quiet weekday), you can skip Express on Day 4 and save ~21,800+ yen/person. If the forecast shows heavy all-day rain on the Arashiyama day, swap Day 6 and Day 7 (do the indoor book/manga day in the rain). If the family prefers deer over books, run the Nara Option B on Day 7.

## Caveats
- **Prices and hours change.** USJ ticket and Express prices are date-based and were rising into 2026; verify on the official calendar. Temple admissions occasionally rise (e.g., Otagi Nenbutsuji rose to 1,000 yen from April 2026).
- **Private-rail fares vary slightly by source** (Hanshin/Hankyu Sannomiya quoted 320 to 330 yen); budget the higher figure.
- **Small shops close one day/week** (often Wed/Mon/Tue) and many are cash-only — confirm before a special trip.
- **USJ early opening is unadvertised** and varies day to day; arriving an hour before posted opening is the safe play.
- **Onsen tattoo policies** differ by facility; if anyone in the group has tattoos, rely on the ryokan's private bath or confirm Kin no Yu's current policy on arrival.
- **June 9 is a Tuesday:** double-check that any Day 8 target isn't on its weekly closure (some museums/temples and Nijo Castle close certain Tuesdays).
